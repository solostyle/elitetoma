Tae Kwon Do literally means "The way of kicking and punching".  Tae Kwon Do was developed in Korea and is known for its powerful and dynamic kicking style.  Students quickly learn, with proper technique, to generate tremendous speed and power.  With a large emphasis on using the legs, the students' balance and coordination improve quickly.

A gradual regime of exercises is used to build flexibility, strength, speed and coordination.  Students learn forms (set sequences of moves or techniques) as well as individual techniques and drills, which improves each student's concentration, reflexes and accuracy.  Sparring against one or more opponents hones a student's skills and increases confidence and control.

 

Hap Ki Do means "the way of coordination and power".  It is a self defense style that utilizes joint locks, pressure points, and throws, as well as kicks and strikes.  Hap Ki Do allows the artist to flow smoothly in and around any opponent, using the opponent's own energy or power against themselves.  This starts out as passive resistance but becomes very aggressive as the opponent's energy increases.  When performed with the proper technique, a small person can overcome and attacker of any size with little effort.

 

Each of these styles, Tae Kwon Do and Hap Ki Do, are excellent in and of themselves.  By training in both styles concurrently, the student develops a well-rounded technique encompassing the power of Tae Kwon Do and the graceful flowing control of Hap Ki Do.
